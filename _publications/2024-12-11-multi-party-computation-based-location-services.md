This manuscript studies a practical privacy problem: how can a service answer nearest-neighbor style queries, like finding nearby results, without learning the user's private input or exposing the server's dataset? The paper uses secure multi-party computation and function secret sharing to design a protocol for private nearest-neighbor search with exact answers.

For undergraduate CS students, this is a strong example of theory meeting systems. It connects algorithms, cryptography, and real-world applications like private search and location services, while also showing how performance matters when turning a secure protocol into something people could actually use.
