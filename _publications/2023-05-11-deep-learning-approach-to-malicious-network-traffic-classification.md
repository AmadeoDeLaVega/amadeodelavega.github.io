We explore whether neural networks can spot malicious network traffic more effectively than rule-based filtering alone. Instead of relying only on hand-crafted firewall signals, we study how to represent packet payloads as model inputs and train deep learning systems to classify traffic as benign or malicious.

This manuscript is a useful bridge between cybersecurity and machine learning. It shows how raw network data becomes features, how model design choices affect real-world deployment, and why reproducibility and dataset quality matter just as much as model accuracy.
