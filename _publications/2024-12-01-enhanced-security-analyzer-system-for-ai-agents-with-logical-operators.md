This manuscript looks at a timely question in AI safety: how do you stop tool-using AI agents from taking unsafe actions when the danger is spread across several steps, repeated events, or missing checks? The paper extends a policy language with logical operators such as NOT, COUNT, NEAR, and WITHIN, making it easier to express and detect more realistic security violations.

For undergraduate CS students, this is a clear example of formal reasoning applied to modern AI systems. It shows how ideas from logic and programming languages can turn vague security concerns into precise rules that an analyzer can check automatically.
